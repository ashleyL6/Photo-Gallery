Hiking the Cascades

The Cascades is a popular hike about 30 minutes away from Virginia Tech. It provides great views of trees turning different colors, pleasant creeks along the way and a waterfall at the end.

Cascades Waterfall

At the end of hiking the Cascades, there's a large waterfall and swimming area where people often swim, picnic and experience the great outdoors.

German Shepard on the Drillfield

A student was walking along the drillfield with his German Shepard puppy, training him to sit and be nice to students who were walking past.

The Start of Fall

This was the first colorful pile of leaves from a tree that I saw at the beginning of fall. The tree was located right next to Shanks Hall.

Colorful Leaves on Hokiestone

By the cadet dormitory, I saw a beautiful, brightly-colored yellow leaf propped up next to Hokiestone, symbolizing the beginning of fall at Virginia Tech

Burruss and a Glimpse of Fall

Burruss Hall peaks out behind one of the first trees on campus to start turning colors in the beginning of October.

Squires Student Center Mimics Fall Colors

With the already vibrant umbrellas outside Au Bon Pain and Burger '37, the fall colors only added to the brilliant yellow image students and faculty see as they pass by Squires.

Thankful for Fall

With the holiday season and Thanksgiving approaching, fall decorations remind us to be thankful for what we have.

Virginia Tech Chair at Sunset

Football season is in full swing during the fall semester, and remnants of tailgating and parties with friends are often left outside as night approches.

Pool at the Retreat Reflects Fall Sunset

Blacksburg's apartment complex called the Retreat is home to a beautiful pool that gets lit up at night and adds to an already stunning scene as one looks out over the mountains.

Flying into Lane

The first night game of the season involved some exciting opening ceremonies. One of the traditions is the Virginia Tech Skydiving Team flying into Lane stadium with the american flag before the game starts.

Fall Sprouts at Virginia Tech

At the end of September and the beginning of October, it's difficult to find trees with a lot of changing colors. However, glimpses of fall can be seen in leaves that sprout from the trunks of trees that will soon take on fall colors.
